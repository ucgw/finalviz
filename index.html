Magnitude 6+ Earthquake Events

Recording Station Counts Effect on Error Ratios

Earthquake magnitudes and depth measurments have been tracked for nearly 100 years in order to help us understand past trends that lead to future enhancements in early detection systems. However, just as important as the measure itself is the error rates associated.

Assembled are 2 charts with multiple drill down scenes revealing both high and low density events around error ratios for both magitude and depth of major earthquakes (6+ magnitude). The data covers the last 5 years, but a few events from very early on when recording stations were being first deployed helps to draw the intended correlations between number of recording stations and the error to actual value ratios of each event.
